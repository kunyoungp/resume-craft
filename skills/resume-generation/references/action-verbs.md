# Action Verbs by Category

Use these categorized action verb lists when writing resume bullet points. Select verbs that match the nature of the work described. Use 20-30 different verbs across a one-page resume. Never start two consecutive bullets with the same verb.

---

## Leadership / Management

Led, Directed, Orchestrated, Oversaw, Mentored, Championed, Coordinated, Guided, Supervised, Managed, Shaped, Grew

Use these when describing team leadership, people management, project ownership, or strategic direction. "Led" is strong and direct. "Orchestrated" implies coordinating many moving parts. "Mentored" shows investment in others' growth. "Championed" signals advocacy for an idea or initiative.

---

## Technical / Engineering

Engineered, Architected, Integrated, Debugged, Optimized, Deployed, Automated, Built, Developed, Designed, Configured, Migrated, Refactored, Scaled

Use these for hands-on technical work. "Built" and "Developed" are versatile. "Architected" implies system-level design decisions. "Migrated" is specific to moving systems between platforms or versions. "Refactored" signals code quality improvement. "Scaled" implies performance work under load.

---

## Analysis / Strategy

Analyzed, Synthesized, Forecasted, Benchmarked, Evaluated, Modeled, Assessed, Identified, Mapped, Researched, Diagnosed, Measured

Use these for analytical, research, or strategic work. "Analyzed" is the workhorse. "Synthesized" implies combining multiple data sources. "Benchmarked" means comparing against standards or competitors. "Diagnosed" works well for troubleshooting and root cause analysis.

---

## Sales / Business Development

Grew, Negotiated, Acquired, Converted, Expanded, Closed, Secured, Partnered, Pitched, Won

Use these for revenue-generating, client-facing, or business development work. "Grew" is versatile (grew revenue, grew accounts, grew territory). "Closed" implies deal completion. "Secured" works for partnerships, funding, or contracts. "Won" is strong for competitive situations.

---

## Creative / Communications

Designed, Crafted, Produced, Curated, Launched, Branded, Published, Wrote, Edited, Presented, Translated

Use these for design, content, communications, or creative work. "Crafted" implies care and intentionality. "Curated" works for content selection or collection building. "Launched" implies bringing something to market or public. "Translated" can mean language translation or translating complex ideas for different audiences.

---

## Operations / Process

Reduced, Eliminated, Improved, Accelerated, Simplified, Consolidated, Restructured, Standardized, Implemented, Executed

Use these for operational improvements, process changes, or efficiency gains. "Reduced" and "Eliminated" are powerful when paired with metrics (cost, time, errors). "Simplified" implies removing unnecessary complexity. "Consolidated" means combining multiple things into one. "Standardized" implies creating consistency.

---

## Financial

Saved, Generated, Increased, Decreased, Budgeted, Allocated, Projected, Audited

Use these for financial impact, budget management, or cost-related work. Always pair with dollar amounts or percentages. "Saved" implies cost reduction. "Generated" implies revenue creation. "Budgeted" and "Allocated" imply financial planning responsibility.

---

## Quantification Templates

Pair action verbs with these quantification patterns to create complete, impactful bullet points:

### Percentage
"Increased user engagement by 25% within 3 months"
"Reduced customer churn by 18% through targeted retention campaigns"
"Improved test coverage from 45% to 92% across 3 microservices"

### Dollar Amounts
"Managed $2.4M annual operating budget"
"Generated $500K in new revenue from enterprise upsell program"
"Saved $180K annually by renegotiating vendor contracts"

### Efficiency
"Reduced system downtime from 4 hours/month to 15 minutes"
"Cut deployment time from 2 hours to 8 minutes with automated CI/CD pipeline"
"Shortened onboarding time from 3 weeks to 4 days with new training program"

### Scale
"Optimized platform serving 10M+ daily active users"
"Built data pipeline processing 2TB of events per day"
"Maintained 99.97% uptime for services handling 50K requests per second"

### Time
"Delivered project 3 weeks ahead of 6-month deadline"
"Reduced average ticket resolution time from 48 hours to 6 hours"
"Completed migration 2 months ahead of schedule with zero data loss"

### Volume
"Processed 50,000+ customer support tickets annually"
"Reviewed and merged 200+ pull requests per quarter"
"Handled 1,500+ inbound sales inquiries monthly"

### Team
"Managed team of 12 engineers across 3 time zones"
"Grew engineering team from 4 to 22 over 18 months"
"Mentored 6 junior developers, 4 of whom were promoted within a year"

### Customer
"Maintained 4.8/5.0 satisfaction rating across 1,200+ reviews"
"Grew NPS score from 32 to 67 within one year"
"Supported 500+ enterprise clients with combined ARR of $15M"

## Usage Rules

1. **Match verb to achievement** — Do not use "Engineered" for a non-technical task or "Negotiated" for a purely technical one.
2. **Rotate categories** — A resume that uses only leadership verbs implies the candidate does not do hands-on work. Mix categories to show range.
3. **Avoid repetition** — Count verb usage. If "Led" appears 4 times, replace 2-3 instances with "Directed," "Coordinated," or "Managed."
4. **Pair every verb with a metric** — An action verb without a quantified result is only half a bullet point. Always complete the pattern: Action Verb + Task/Project + Quantified Result.
